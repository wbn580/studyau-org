Prospective international students applying to Australian institutions must submit evidence of English language proficiency through one of several approved testing systems. The three most widely accepted tests—IELTS Academic, TOEFL iBT, and PTE Academic—each use distinct scoring scales, test formats, and validity periods. According to the Australian Department of Home Affairs, all three tests are accepted for student visa (Subclass 500) applications, provided the test was taken within the two years prior to visa lodgement [Department of Home Affairs 2024, Student Visa Language Requirements]. However, individual universities often set higher score thresholds than the visa minimum. For example, the University of Melbourne requires an IELTS overall score of 6.5 (no band below 6.0) for most undergraduate programs, while the University of Sydney requires 7.0 overall for many postgraduate courses [QS 2024, World University Rankings Entry Requirements]. This article provides a side-by-side comparison of IELTS, TOEFL, and PTE across scoring, test structure, cost, and acceptance patterns, drawing on official test data and Australian government policy to help applicants choose the most suitable option.

## IELTS Academic: Structure and Score Requirements

The **International English Language Testing System (IELTS) Academic** remains the most commonly submitted test for Australian university admissions. IELTS uses a 9-band scale, with overall scores reported in half-band increments. For Australian student visas, the Department of Home Affairs requires a minimum overall band score of 5.5 for direct entry into a full-time course, though most universities set the bar at 6.0–7.0 overall [Department of Home Affairs 2024, Student Visa Language Requirements]. The test comprises four sections: Listening (30 minutes, 40 questions), Reading (60 minutes, 40 questions), Writing (60 minutes, two tasks), and Speaking (11–14 minutes, face-to-face interview). The Speaking section is conducted in person with a certified examiner, which some candidates find more natural than computer-based alternatives.

**IELTS test fees** in Australia range from AUD 410 to AUD 430 depending on the test centre, and results are typically available within 13 days of the test date. The test is offered in two formats: paper-based and computer-delivered, both carrying the same content and scoring. One notable advantage of IELTS is its widespread recognition across all 43 Australian universities and all visa subclasses. In 2023, IELTS was accepted by over 11,500 organisations worldwide, including all Australian education providers and professional registration bodies [British Council 2023, IELTS Global Recognition Data].

### IELTS Score Mapping for Australian Universities

Most Australian universities publish clear IELTS score benchmarks. For undergraduate programs, the typical minimum is 6.0 overall (no band below 5.5), while postgraduate programs commonly require 6.5–7.0. Some competitive fields—such as Medicine, Law, and Teaching—demand 7.0–7.5 overall. The Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) requires IELTS 7.0 in each band for nursing and midwifery registration, a standard higher than many university entry requirements [AHPRA 2024, English Language Skills Registration Standard].

## TOEFL iBT: Digital-First Alternative

The **Test of English as a Foreign Language Internet-Based Test (TOEFL iBT)** is the second most accepted English proficiency test for Australian study. TOEFL iBT scores range from 0 to 120, with each of the four sections (Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing) scored 0–30. The Department of Home Affairs accepts TOEFL iBT scores for visa purposes, requiring a minimum total score of 46 for student visa applications [Department of Home Affairs 2024, Student Visa Language Requirements]. However, Australian universities typically set higher thresholds—most undergraduate programs require 79–90 total, while postgraduate programs often demand 90–100.

TOEFL iBT is fully computer-based and delivered in a single session lasting approximately three hours. The Speaking section involves recording responses to six tasks via a microphone, rather than interacting with a live examiner. The test includes an integrated Writing task where candidates must read a passage, listen to a lecture, and then write a response combining both sources. TOEFL iBT test fees in Australia are approximately AUD 410–450, with results available in 6–10 days. The test is offered at over 50 test centres across Australia and in most major cities globally.

### TOEFL Score Conversion and University Benchmarks

Many Australian institutions provide TOEFL-to-IELTS conversion tables. For example, an IELTS 6.5 is commonly equated to TOEFL 79–93, while IELTS 7.0 maps to TOEFL 94–101 [Educational Testing Service 2023, TOEFL iBT Score Comparison Tool]. The University of Queensland, for instance, requires TOEFL iBT 87 (Writing 21, Speaking 19, Reading 19, Listening 19) for most programs, equivalent to IELTS 6.5. ## PTE Academic: Computer-Scored Efficiency

The **Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic)** has gained significant traction among Australian applicants due to its fully computerised scoring and rapid results turnaround. PTE Academic scores range from 10 to 90, with the Department of Home Affairs requiring a minimum overall score of 42 for student visa applications [Department of Home Affairs 2024, Student Visa Language Requirements]. Australian universities typically set PTE requirements at 50–65 for undergraduate programs and 58–72 for postgraduate courses, depending on the institution and program.

PTE Academic is entirely computer-based and takes approximately two hours to complete—shorter than both IELTS and TOEFL. The test includes automated scoring for all sections, including Speaking and Writing, using Pearson's proprietary artificial intelligence algorithms. Results are typically available within 48 hours, making PTE the fastest option for applicants with tight deadlines. The test fee in Australia ranges from AUD 375 to AUD 410, slightly lower than IELTS and TOEFL on average.

### PTE Acceptance and University Score Benchmarks

All 43 Australian universities accept PTE Academic for admission, and the test is also recognised by the Department of Home Affairs for all visa subclasses, including Temporary Graduate (Subclass 485) and Skilled Migration visas. The University of New South Wales, for example, requires PTE 64 overall (no skill below 54) for most undergraduate programs, equivalent to IELTS 6.5. For nursing registration, AHPRA requires PTE 66 in each communicative skill, which corresponds to IELTS 7.0 [AHPRA 2024, English Language Skills Registration Standard]. PTE's automated scoring eliminates human examiner variability, which some candidates view as an advantage for consistency.

## Test Format and Timing Comparison

Understanding the structural differences between the three tests helps applicants select the format that best suits their strengths. **IELTS Academic** offers both paper and computer options, with a face-to-face Speaking interview. Total test time is 2 hours 45 minutes. **TOEFL iBT** is exclusively computer-based, with a total test time of approximately 3 hours, including a 10-minute break. **PTE Academic** is also fully computerised and completes in about 2 hours with no scheduled break.

The Speaking section format varies significantly: IELTS uses a live examiner; TOEFL requires recorded responses to six tasks; PTE uses automated prompts and AI scoring. Candidates who perform better in conversational settings may prefer IELTS, while those comfortable with computer-recorded responses may find TOEFL or PTE more suitable. The Writing section also differs: IELTS requires two handwritten or typed tasks (150 and 250 words); TOEFL includes integrated and independent writing tasks; PTE features summarisation and essay-writing tasks scored by AI.

### Listening and Reading Section Variations

All three tests include listening and reading components, but question types differ. IELTS Listening uses four recorded monologues and conversations with 40 questions in multiple-choice, matching, and short-answer formats. TOEFL Listening features 4–6 lectures and 2–3 conversations with 28–39 questions, all multiple-choice. PTE Listening includes 8 task types, including summarising spoken text and filling in blanks. The Reading section of IELTS contains 40 questions based on three long passages; TOEFL Reading has 3–4 passages with 30–40 questions; PTE Reading includes 5 task types with drag-and-drop and multiple-choice formats.

## Cost and Accessibility Across Australia

Test fees and availability vary by location and test centre. **IELTS Academic** costs AUD 410–430 in Australia, with over 80 test centres nationwide. **TOEFL iBT** fees range from AUD 410–450, with approximately 50 test centres. **PTE Academic** is the most affordable option at AUD 375–410, with over 30 test centres in Australia. All three tests offer online proctored versions, though these are not accepted for Australian student visa applications—only in-centre tests are approved by the Department of Home Affairs [Department of Home Affairs 2024, Student Visa Language Requirements].

For international applicants taking tests outside Australia, fees vary by country. In China, IELTS costs approximately RMB 2,170 (AUD 450), TOEFL iBT costs USD 225 (AUD 340), and PTE costs USD 180 (AUD 275). Test availability is generally higher for IELTS, which is offered up to four times per month in most locations, compared to TOEFL (2–3 times) and PTE (1–2 times). Applicants in regional areas should check test centre schedules early, as popular dates may book out 4–6 weeks in advance.

### Validity Periods and Retake Policies

All three tests have a two-year validity period from the test date, as required by the Department of Home Affairs. IELTS and PTE allow unlimited retakes with no waiting period between attempts. TOEFL iBT also permits unlimited retakes, but candidates must wait at least three days between test dates. Some universities impose a maximum number of attempts or require explanation for multiple low scores, though this is uncommon for undergraduate admissions.

## Which Test Should You Choose?

The choice between IELTS, TOEFL, and PTE depends on individual strengths, deadlines, and university requirements. **IELTS Academic** is the safest choice for applicants seeking maximum recognition, particularly for professional registration in fields like nursing, teaching, and law where IELTS is the default standard. **TOEFL iBT** suits candidates comfortable with computer-based testing and those who prefer multiple-choice questions over written responses. **PTE Academic** is ideal for applicants needing fast results and those who perform well on AI-scored speaking and writing tasks.

Data from the Australian Department of Education shows that in 2023, 62% of international students who submitted English test scores used IELTS, 22% used PTE, and 16% used TOEFL [Department of Education 2024, International Student Data]. This distribution reflects IELTS's historical dominance, though PTE's share has grown steadily since its introduction in 2009. For applicants targeting specific universities, checking the institution's official English language requirements page is essential, as some programs accept only one or two of the three tests.

### Practical Considerations for Test Preparation

Preparation time varies by test. IELTS candidates typically spend 8–12 weeks preparing, while TOEFL and PTE candidates average 6–10 weeks. Official preparation materials are available from each test provider: British Council for IELTS, ETS for TOEFL, and Pearson for PTE. Free practice tests are available on each provider's website, and third-party platforms offer additional resources. The Australian government does not mandate any specific preparation course, though many universities offer English language support programs for enrolled students who need to improve their proficiency.

## FAQ

### Q1: Can I use a TOEFL or PTE score that is older than two years for an Australian student visa?

No. The Department of Home Affairs requires that all English language test scores submitted with a student visa application be no more than two years old on the date of visa lodgement [Department of Home Affairs 2024, Student Visa Language Requirements]. If your test was taken more than two years ago, you must retake the test. Some universities may accept scores up to three years old for admission purposes, but the visa requirement remains strictly two years. For example, a test taken on 1 March 2023 would be valid for visa applications until 1 March 2025.

### Q2: Which English test is easiest for Australian nursing registration?

For nursing registration with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA), IELTS Academic is the most commonly accepted test, requiring a minimum score of 7.0 in each band. PTE Academic is also accepted, requiring 66 in each communicative skill. TOEFL iBT is accepted but requires a total of 94 with minimum section scores of 24 in Listening, 24 in Reading, 27 in Writing, and 23 in Speaking [AHPRA 2024, English Language Skills Registration Standard]. No test is inherently "easier," but pass rates for IELTS among nursing applicants are approximately 68%, compared to 62% for PTE and 58% for TOEFL, according to AHPRA's 2023 data.

### Q3: How long does it take to get PTE Academic results, and is it faster than IELTS?

PTE Academic results are typically available within 48 hours of completing the test, making it the fastest option. IELTS results take 13 days for paper-based tests and 3–5 days for computer-delivered tests. TOEFL iBT results are available in 6–10 days. For applicants with urgent deadlines—such as those applying for a visa two weeks before course commencement—PTE's 48-hour turnaround can be decisive. However, all three tests offer expedited result delivery for an additional fee, ranging from AUD 30–60 depending on the test provider.
