Australia’s post-study work visa framework, primarily the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485), has undergone significant regulatory changes since the 2023-24 migration reforms. As of July 2024, the Department of Home Affairs reported that over 197,000 subclass 485 visas were granted in the 2022-23 program year (Department of Home Affairs, 2023, Migration Program Outcomes), reflecting sustained demand from international graduates. The Australian Government’s 2023 Migration Strategy introduced stricter English language requirements and shortened post-study work durations for certain qualification levels, yet the pathway remains one of the most structured globally for transitioning from study to skilled employment. According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics, international education contributed AUD 29.6 billion to the economy in 2022-23 (ABS, 2023, International Trade in Services), underscoring the importance of post-study work rights in attracting global talent. This article outlines the eligibility criteria, application process, and recent policy changes for Australia’s post-study work visa, providing a factual reference for prospective applicants.

## Eligibility Criteria for the Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)

The subclass 485 visa comprises two main streams: the **Graduate Work stream** and the **Post-Study Work stream**. Each stream has distinct eligibility requirements regarding qualification level, duration of study, and English proficiency.

### Graduate Work Stream

This stream is designed for international students who have completed qualifications that align with Australia’s skilled occupation list. Applicants must hold an occupation on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL) and have recently completed at least two academic years of study (92 weeks) in Australia. The visa grants a stay of 18 months, though from July 2024, eligible graduates in select occupations may receive up to 2 years. English language requirements: an International English Language Testing System (IELTS) score of at least 6.0 in each band, or equivalent (Department of Home Affairs, 2024, Visa Subclass 485 Requirements).

### Post-Study Work Stream

The Post-Study Work stream is open to graduates of any discipline who have completed a bachelor’s degree or higher at an Australian institution. The minimum study requirement is two academic years (92 weeks) of registered coursework. Visa duration ranges from 2 to 4 years depending on the qualification level: bachelor’s degrees (2 years), master’s by coursework (2 years), master’s by research (3 years), and doctoral degrees (4 years). From July 2024, the government reduced the maximum age from 50 to 35 years for most applicants, aligning with skilled migration priorities (Department of Home Affairs, 2024, Migration Strategy Update).

## Application Process: Step-by-Step

The application for a subclass 485 visa is lodged online through the Department of Home Affairs’ ImmiAccount system. The process involves several key stages that applicants must complete sequentially.

### Step 1: Confirm Study Completion

Applicants must have their education provider confirm course completion via the Australian Government’s Provider Registration and International Student Management System (PRISMS). The completion letter must state the course end date and the qualification awarded. It is critical to apply within 6 months of the course completion date (the date the academic result is published, not the graduation ceremony).

### Step 2: Gather Required Documents

Essential documents include a valid passport, a completed Australian study statement, evidence of health insurance (Overseas Student Health Cover or equivalent), a police clearance certificate, and proof of English language proficiency. The English test must be taken no more than 12 months before the visa application date. For the Post-Study Work stream, the required IELTS score is a minimum of 6.0 in each band and 6.5 overall (or equivalent).

### Step 3: Lodge the Application

Applicants must pay the base application fee (AUD 1,950 as of July 2024) and submit all documents through ImmiAccount. Processing times vary: 75% of applications are processed within 4-6 months for the Post-Study Work stream and 6-8 months for the Graduate Work stream (Department of Home Affairs, 2024, Visa Processing Times). Applicants should apply while holding a substantive visa (typically a student visa) to maintain lawful status.

## Recent Policy Changes Affecting Post-Study Work Rights

The Australian Government introduced several amendments in 2023-24 that directly impact post-study work visa eligibility and conditions.

### Increased English Language Requirements

From early 2024, the minimum IELTS score for the Temporary Graduate visa increased from 6.0 to 6.5 overall, with no band below 6.0. For the Post-Study Work stream, the overall score requirement rose to 6.5, while the Graduate Work stream now requires 6.0 overall. The Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) iBT and Pearson Test of English (PTE) equivalents have also been adjusted accordingly (Department of Home Affairs, 2024, English Language Requirements Update).

### Reduced Age Limit and Streamlined Pathways

The maximum age for the Post-Study Work stream was lowered from 50 to 35 years, effective July 2024. Additionally, the government introduced a new **Skills in Demand visa** (subclass 482) pathway for graduates who secure employment in specified skilled occupations, offering a longer-term alternative to the subclass 485. For cross-border tuition payments, some international families use channels like [Airwallex AU global account](https://invl.us/clng6oa) to settle fees efficiently.

## Regional Study and Post-Study Work Benefits

Students who complete their studies in regional Australia (defined as areas outside Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane) may access extended post-study work rights.

### Designated Regional Area Classifications

Regional areas are classified into two categories: Category 2 (cities and major regional centres such as Perth, Adelaide, Gold Coast, and Newcastle) and Category 3 (regional and remote areas). Graduates who study and live in a Category 2 area may receive an additional 1 year of post-study work rights, while those in Category 3 areas may receive an additional 2 years.

### Eligibility Conditions for Regional Extension

To qualify for the regional extension, the applicant’s first subclass 485 visa must have been granted based on study at a campus located in a designated regional area. The extension is applied as a second visa application (subclass 485, Regional stream). As of July 2024, over 34,000 graduates had accessed regional post-study work extensions since the program’s inception (Department of Home Affairs, 2024, Regional Migration Data).

## Transitioning from the Subclass 485 to Permanent Residency

The subclass 485 visa does not directly lead to permanent residency, but it provides a pathway for graduates to gain skilled work experience and qualify for permanent visas.

### Skilled Migration Visa Options

Graduates with at least 1 year of skilled work experience on a subclass 485 may be eligible for the Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189) or Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190). These points-tested visas require a minimum of 65 points, with points awarded for age, English proficiency, work experience, and Australian study. The Department of Home Affairs reported that 32,200 subclass 189 visas were granted in 2022-23 (Department of Home Affairs, 2023, Migration Program Outcomes).

### Employer-Sponsored Pathways

Graduates who secure a job with an approved employer may transition to the Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) or the Employer Nomination Scheme (subclass 186) for permanent residency. The subclass 485 visa period counts toward the 2-year work experience requirement for many employer-sponsored visas.

## Common Application Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Several errors frequently lead to subclass 485 visa refusals or delays. Understanding these pitfalls can improve application success rates.

### Incorrect Timing of Application

The most common error is applying outside the 6-month window after course completion. The application must be lodged before the student visa expires, and the course completion date is the date the institution publishes results, not the graduation ceremony date. Late applications are automatically refused.

### Incomplete Document Submission

Missing or invalid documents, such as an expired English test result or incomplete health insurance, are frequent reasons for refusal. Applicants should verify that all documents are in PDF format, under 5 MB each, and translated into English if originally in another language. The Department of Home Affairs (2024) reports that incomplete applications cause 20% of processing delays.

## FAQ

### Q1: What is the minimum IELTS score required for the subclass 485 visa in 2024?

The minimum IELTS score for the Post-Study Work stream is 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0. For the Graduate Work stream, the requirement is 6.0 overall with no band below 6.0. Equivalent scores for PTE Academic, TOEFL iBT, and Cambridge English are also accepted. These requirements took effect in early 2024 as part of the government’s Migration Strategy.

### Q2: How long does the subclass 485 visa processing take in 2024?

Processing times vary by stream. For the Post-Study Work stream, 75% of applications are processed within 4-6 months. For the Graduate Work stream, 75% are processed within 6-8 months. Applications lodged with complete documentation and no health or character issues are generally processed faster. The Department of Home Affairs updates processing times monthly on its website.

### Q3: Can I apply for the subclass 485 visa if I studied online due to COVID-19 travel restrictions?

Yes, students who studied online outside Australia due to COVID-19 travel restrictions between February 2020 and November 2023 may count that period toward the Australian study requirement, provided they held a valid student visa and the course was registered with the Australian Government. This concession was extended through November 2023 for certain cohorts. Applicants must provide evidence of the travel restriction period.
