The University of Sydney (USyd) recorded 74,434 enrolled students in 2023, of whom 29,665 were international students from over 170 countries, according to the university’s 2023 Annual Report. As Australia’s first university, founded in 1850, it consistently ranks among the world’s top 40 institutions—placing 28th in the QS World University Rankings 2025 and 60th in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2024. This guide provides a detailed breakdown of USyd’s flagship programs, their curriculum structures, and what prospective international students should know before applying.

## Architecture, Design and Planning: The Envelope of Urban Innovation

USyd’s **Bachelor of Architecture and Environments** and **Master of Architecture** are accredited by the Architects Accreditation Council of Australia (AACA) and the NSW Architects Registration Board. The undergraduate program spans three years, covering design studios, building technology, and architectural history, with a strong emphasis on digital fabrication and sustainable design. The two-year Master’s degree requires a portfolio and focuses on advanced design research, urban analytics, and professional practice.

**Curriculum structure**: The Bachelor’s includes core units such as Architecture Studio 1–6 (each 6 credit points), Building Science (6 cp), and History of World Architecture (6 cp). Students must complete 144 credit points in total, with 24 cp of electives from other faculties. The Master’s requires 96 cp, including a final design thesis (24 cp) and a professional practice unit (6 cp). The faculty maintains a 12:1 student-to-staff ratio, allowing intensive studio-based learning.

Career outcomes: Graduates work at firms like Foster + Partners, Hassell, and Woods Bagot. The median starting salary for architecture graduates is AUD 65,000, per the Graduate Outcomes Survey 2023. For cross-border tuition payments, some international families use channels like [Airwallex AU global account](https://invl.us/clng6oa) to settle fees in multiple currencies.

## Business and Commerce: A Gateway to Global Markets

The **University of Sydney Business School** holds triple accreditation from AACSB, AMBA, and EQUIS—a distinction held by fewer than 1% of business schools worldwide. The **Bachelor of Commerce** offers 12 majors, including Finance, Accounting, Marketing, and Business Analytics. The **Master of Commerce** (1.5 years) and **Master of Commerce (Extension)** (2 years) allow specialisation in up to two areas.

**Curriculum structure**: The Bachelor’s requires 144 credit points, with 48 cp of core units (e.g., Business in the Global Economy, Quantitative Methods for Business) and 48 cp of a chosen major. The Master of Commerce (Extension) comprises 96 cp, including a capstone project (6 cp) and a research pathway option. Students can undertake internships with partners like PwC, Deloitte, and Westpac.

Employment data: 89.4% of business graduates found full-time employment within four months of graduation (QILT 2023), with a median salary of AUD 68,000. The Finance major has the highest average starting salary at AUD 72,000.

## Engineering and Computer Science: Building the Digital Backbone

The **Faculty of Engineering** offers **Bachelor of Engineering (Honours)** in 18 streams, including Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, and Software. The **Master of Professional Engineering** is accredited by Engineers Australia under the Washington Accord. The **Master of Computer Science** (1.5 years) and **Master of Data Science** (1.5 years) are designed for graduates with a computing background.

**Curriculum structure**: The BE (Hons) requires 192 credit points over four years, including a 24-cp thesis in the final year. The Master of Professional Engineering (2 years) includes 48 cp of technical coursework and a 24-cp design project. The Master of Computer Science covers algorithms, machine learning, and cybersecurity, with a 12-cp capstone.

Industry partnerships: USyd is part of the **Sydney Quantum Academy** and collaborates with Microsoft, Atlassian, and CSIRO’s Data61. Research income in engineering exceeded AUD 52 million in 2022 (USyd Annual Report 2022). Graduates report a median salary of AUD 75,000, with software engineers averaging AUD 85,000.

## Medicine and Health: Clinical Excellence and Research Impact

The **Sydney Medical School** offers the **Doctor of Medicine (MD)** , a four-year graduate-entry program. It is ranked 18th globally for medicine (QS 2024) and is affiliated with 12 teaching hospitals, including Royal Prince Alfred Hospital and Westmead Hospital. The **Bachelor of Science (Health)** and **Master of Public Health** provide alternative entry points.

**Curriculum structure**: The MD curriculum follows a spiral learning model—foundation sciences in Year 1, clinical rotations in Years 2–3, and a research project or elective in Year 4. Students complete 192 credit points, with 48 cp of clinical placements. Admission requires a bachelor’s degree (minimum GPA 5.0/7.0), GAMSAT score (minimum 50 in each section), and an interview.

**Research output**: USyd’s Faculty of Medicine and Health received AUD 143 million in research grants in 2022 (NHMRC data). The **Charles Perkins Centre** focuses on obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease. Graduates have a 97% employment rate within six months, with a median salary of AUD 82,000 for junior doctors.

## Law: A Foundation for Justice and Advocacy

The **Sydney Law School** is consistently ranked among the top 20 law schools globally (QS 2024). It offers the **Bachelor of Laws (LLB)** as a five-year combined degree (e.g., LLB/Bachelor of Arts, LLB/Bachelor of Commerce) and the **Juris Doctor (JD)** , a three-year graduate-entry program.

**Curriculum structure**: The LLB combined degree requires 240 credit points, including 96 cp of law units (e.g., Torts, Contracts, Criminal Law, Constitutional Law) and a capstone research paper (6 cp). The JD comprises 144 cp, with compulsory units in Legal Research and Writing, and an elective clinical placement at the **Sydney Law School Community Legal Centre**.

Admission requirements: For the LLB, domestic students need an ATAR of 99.5 (NSW) or equivalent; international students require IELTS 7.5 overall (no band below 7.0). The JD requires a bachelor’s degree with a minimum GPA of 5.5/7.0 and LSAT score (recommended 155+). The Law School’s **Moot Court** program has won national and international competitions, including the Philip C. Jessup International Law Moot.

## Arts and Social Sciences: Critical Thinking Across Cultures

The **Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences** offers the **Bachelor of Arts** as a three-year degree with over 40 majors, including International Relations, Economics, and Indigenous Studies. The **Master of International Relations** and **Master of Cultural Studies** are popular postgraduate options.

**Curriculum structure**: The BA requires 144 credit points, with 48 cp of a major sequence and 24 cp of a minor. Students must complete at least 12 cp of a language other than English. The Master of International Relations (1.5 years) covers global governance, security studies, and international political economy, with a 12-cp dissertation option.

**Research strengths**: The faculty hosts the **Sydney Environment Institute** and the **United States Studies Centre**. In the 2023 ERA (Excellence in Research for Australia) assessment, 80% of its research outputs were rated at or above world standard. Graduates find roles in diplomacy, journalism, and non-profit organisations, with a median salary of AUD 62,000.

## Science and Veterinary Science: From Lab Bench to Field

The **Faculty of Science** offers the **Bachelor of Science** (3 years) with 26 majors, including Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Environmental Science. The **Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM)** is a six-year program accredited by the Australasian Veterinary Boards Council (AVBC).

**Curriculum structure**: The BSc requires 144 credit points, including 48 cp of foundational science units and a 12-cp research project in the final year. The DVM comprises 360 credit points, with clinical placements at the **University Veterinary Teaching Hospital** (UVTH) in Camden. Students manage real cases from Year 4 onward.

**Facilities**: The **Sydney Nano Institute** provides AUD 40 million in equipment for nanoscience research. The **One Tree Island Research Station** on the Great Barrier Reef supports marine biology fieldwork. Science graduates have a 91% employment rate, with a median salary of AUD 65,000.

## FAQ

### Q1: What are the English language requirements for the University of Sydney's top programs?

Most programs require IELTS 7.0 overall (no band below 6.5) for undergraduate courses and IELTS 7.5 overall (no band below 7.0) for Law and Medicine. The University of Sydney also accepts TOEFL iBT (minimum 96 for most courses) and PTE Academic (minimum 68). Some programs may waive the requirement for applicants from English-speaking countries or those who completed secondary education in English. Always check the specific program page, as requirements vary by faculty.

### Q2: How much do tuition fees cost for international students at the University of Sydney?

Annual tuition fees for 2024 range from AUD 47,000 (Bachelor of Arts) to AUD 56,000 (Doctor of Medicine) and AUD 53,000 (Bachelor of Engineering). The Master of Commerce costs approximately AUD 54,000 per year. These figures exclude the Student Services and Amenities Fee (AUD 315 per year) and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC), which costs around AUD 600–800 per year for single coverage. Fee increases of 3–5% per annum are typical.

### Q3: What is the application deadline for international students applying to the University of Sydney?

For Semester 1 (February) intake, the application deadline is 15 January for most programs, though Medicine and Law have earlier cutoffs (30 November of the previous year). For Semester 2 (July) intake, the deadline is 30 June. Some high-demand programs like the Doctor of Medicine close applications 12 months before the start date. Conditional offers are common, and applicants should allow 4–6 weeks for processing after submitting all documents.
