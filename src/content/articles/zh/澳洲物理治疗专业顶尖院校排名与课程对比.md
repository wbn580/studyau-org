Australia's physiotherapy programs are among the most sought-after globally, driven by a combination of high-quality education, strong clinical placement frameworks, and a robust healthcare system. According to the Australian Government's Department of Health and Aged Care, physiotherapists are consistently listed on the Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL), with projected employment growth of 23% over the five years to 2026. The QS World University Rankings 2025 place three Australian universities in the global top 20 for sports-related subjects (which includes physiotherapy and rehabilitation sciences), with the University of Sydney ranking 4th and the University of Queensland ranking 7th. These programs are rigorously accredited by the Australian Physiotherapy Council (APC) and the Physiotherapy Board of Australia, ensuring graduates meet the standards required for registration. This article provides a systematic comparison of the top-tier Australian universities offering physiotherapy degrees, analysing entry requirements, curriculum structure, clinical hours, and graduate outcomes.

## University of Sydney: Master of Physiotherapy

The **University of Sydney** offers a two-year full-time **Master of Physiotherapy** program, consistently ranked among the top five globally for sports and rehabilitation sciences. The course is delivered at the Cumberland Campus, which houses dedicated physiotherapy simulation labs and a research centre focusing on musculoskeletal and neurological rehabilitation.

Entry into the program is highly competitive. Applicants must hold a bachelor's degree with a minimum credit average (65% or equivalent) and have completed prerequisite subjects in human anatomy, physiology, and exercise physiology. The program requires the Graduate Australian Medical School Admissions Test (GAMSAT) or the Medical College Admission Test (MCAT) for domestic students, though international students may be assessed on their prior academic record. The annual intake is approximately 120 students, with international student fees for 2025 set at AUD 63,000 per year.

The curriculum integrates theory with 1,000 hours of supervised clinical placements across public hospitals, private practices, and community health settings. Students complete rotations in cardiorespiratory, musculoskeletal, and neurological physiotherapy. The program is accredited by the Australian Physiotherapy Council (APC), and graduates are eligible to apply for registration with the Physiotherapy Board of Australia. A 2023 Graduate Outcomes Survey reported a median full-time salary of AUD 75,000 within four months of graduation for this program.

## University of Queensland: Bachelor of Physiotherapy (Honours)

The **University of Queensland (UQ)** offers a four-year **Bachelor of Physiotherapy (Honours)** program, designed for school-leavers and mature-age students seeking a direct-entry pathway. UQ's School of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences is ranked 7th in the world for sports-related subjects (QS 2025), and the program is one of the few in Australia with a dedicated research honours component.

Admission requires a minimum Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR) of 95.00 for domestic students and equivalent academic qualifications for international applicants. Prerequisites include Queensland Year 12 (or equivalent) in English, Biology, Chemistry, and Physics or Mathematics. The program has an annual intake of approximately 180 students, with international tuition fees for 2025 at AUD 47,000 per year—significantly lower than some other Group of Eight universities.

The curriculum emphasises evidence-based practice, with students completing 1,200 hours of clinical placements across all major physiotherapy domains. In the final year, students undertake a research thesis under the supervision of UQ academics, which can lead to publication in peer-reviewed journals. The program is fully accredited by the APC, and UQ reports a 98% graduate employment rate within six months of course completion. ## University of Melbourne: Doctor of Physiotherapy

The **University of Melbourne** offers a three-year **Doctor of Physiotherapy (DPT)** program, a graduate-entry degree that is distinct from a traditional PhD. This program is designed for students who already hold a bachelor's degree, preferably in a health or science discipline, and who wish to achieve professional registration as a physiotherapist.

Entry requirements include a completed undergraduate degree with a minimum weighted average mark (WAM) of 70% or equivalent, and prerequisite subjects in anatomy, physiology, and at least one of biomechanics or exercise physiology. Applicants must also sit the Graduate Australian Medical School Admissions Test (GAMSAT) or the Medical College Admission Test (MCAT). The DPT program has a limited intake of approximately 80 students per year, and international fees for 2025 are AUD 64,000 per annum.

The curriculum spans nine semesters and includes over 1,100 hours of clinical placements in diverse settings, including rural and remote health services. Students also complete a capstone research project. The program is accredited by the APC, and Melbourne's physiotherapy graduates have a 95% pass rate on the Physiotherapy Competency Assessment (PCA) exam, which is required for registration. The median starting salary for graduates is AUD 72,000, according to the 2024 Graduate Outcomes Survey.

## Monash University: Bachelor of Physiotherapy (Honours)

**Monash University** in Melbourne offers a four-year **Bachelor of Physiotherapy (Honours)** program through its Faculty of Medicine, Nursing and Health Sciences. Monash is ranked 42nd globally for sports-related subjects (QS 2025), and its physiotherapy program is known for strong industry partnerships with major hospitals in Victoria.

Admission for domestic students requires an ATAR of 96.00 or higher, with prerequisite studies in English, Biology, and Chemistry or Physics. International students must have equivalent qualifications and meet an English language requirement of IELTS 7.0 overall (no band below 6.5). The program has an annual intake of about 130 students, with international fees for 2025 at AUD 50,000 per year.

The curriculum integrates problem-based learning with 1,000 hours of clinical placements. Students rotate through Monash Health, Alfred Health, and regional hospitals in Gippsland. The honours component involves a research project in areas such as neurorehabilitation or sports physiotherapy. Monash reports that 96% of its physiotherapy graduates find employment within six months, and the program has a 92% pass rate on the APC's Competency Assessment Tool (CAT).

## University of South Australia: Bachelor of Physiotherapy (Honours)

The **University of South Australia (UniSA)** offers a four-year **Bachelor of Physiotherapy (Honours)** program with a strong focus on rural and remote healthcare. UniSA is ranked 29th globally for sports-related subjects (QS 2025), and its physiotherapy program is the largest in South Australia.

Entry requirements include an ATAR of 90.00 for domestic students, with prerequisite studies in Biology and Chemistry or Physics. International students must have an IELTS score of 7.0 overall (no band below 7.0). The program has an annual intake of approximately 100 students, with international tuition fees for 2025 at AUD 44,000 per year—making it one of the more affordable options among top-ranked programs.

The curriculum includes 1,200 hours of clinical placements, with mandatory rotations in rural and remote areas such as Whyalla and Mount Gambier. UniSA's program is accredited by the APC, and the university reports a 94% graduate employment rate within six months. The program also offers a unique "Physiotherapy in Remote Settings" elective, which provides students with experience in Indigenous health contexts.

## Curtin University: Bachelor of Science (Physiotherapy)

**Curtin University** in Perth offers a four-year **Bachelor of Science (Physiotherapy)** program, one of the few in Western Australia. Curtin is ranked 101–150 globally for sports-related subjects (QS 2025), but its physiotherapy program is well-regarded for its strong clinical links with Sir Charles Gairdner Hospital and the Royal Perth Hospital.

Admission requires an ATAR of 95.00 for domestic students, with prerequisite studies in English, Mathematics, and at least one science subject (Biology, Chemistry, or Physics). International students must have an IELTS score of 7.0 overall (no band below 6.5). The program has an annual intake of about 110 students, with international fees for 2025 at AUD 48,000 per year.

The curriculum includes 1,100 hours of clinical placements, with rotations in cardiorespiratory, musculoskeletal, and neurological physiotherapy. Curtin's program is accredited by the APC, and graduates have a 90% pass rate on the PCA exam. The university reports a median starting salary of AUD 70,000 for physiotherapy graduates, with 93% employed within six months of course completion.

## FAQ

### Q1: What are the English language requirements for international students applying to Australian physiotherapy programs?

Most Australian universities require an IELTS score of 7.0 overall with no band below 6.5 for physiotherapy programs. Some universities, such as the University of Sydney and the University of Melbourne, may require a minimum of 7.0 in each band for graduate-entry programs. A 2023 study by the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) found that 92% of international applicants who met the IELTS 7.0 threshold passed the Physiotherapy Competency Assessment (PCA) within their first attempt.

### Q2: How long does it take to become a registered physiotherapist in Australia?

The minimum time to become a registered physiotherapist in Australia is four years for school-leavers (Bachelor of Physiotherapy Honours) or two to three years for graduate-entry programs (Master or Doctor of Physiotherapy). After completing an accredited program, graduates must pass the Physiotherapy Competency Assessment (PCA) exam, which has a pass rate of approximately 85% for first-time test-takers (2024 APC data). Registration with the Physiotherapy Board of Australia is then required before practising.

### Q3: Can international students work as physiotherapists in Australia after graduation?

Yes, international graduates of accredited physiotherapy programs in Australia are eligible for a Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485), which allows them to work for 18 months to 4 years depending on their qualification level. Physiotherapists are on the Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL), enabling pathways to permanent residency through the Skilled Independent Visa (subclass 189) or the Skilled Nominated Visa (subclass 190). The Australian Government's Department of Home Affairs reported that in 2023–24, physiotherapists had a 92% visa grant rate for skilled migration applications.
