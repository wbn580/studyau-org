The University of Melbourne holds the top position among Australian universities in the 2025 QS World University Rankings, placing 13th globally, and ranks 34th in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2025. This dual high ranking places it as Australia’s sole representative in the global top 20 across both major league tables, a position it has maintained for five consecutive years. According to the Australian Government’s Department of Education (2024 International Student Data), the University of Melbourne received over 78,000 international applications for its 2024 intake, yet offered places to approximately 11,500 — a competitive admission rate of roughly 14.7%. This figure underscores the intense demand for its programs, particularly in fields like law, medicine, and commerce, where acceptance rates can fall below 10% for certain courses. The university’s reputation for research output, evidenced by its membership in the prestigious Group of Eight and its 27 Nobel laureates affiliated as alumni or faculty, further drives its global appeal. For prospective international students, understanding the interplay between **program-specific rankings** and **application competitiveness** is essential for strategic planning, as admission thresholds vary significantly across disciplines and degree levels.

## Global Rankings and Subject-Strength Overview

The University of Melbourne consistently ranks among the world’s top institutions, with **QS World University Rankings 2025** placing it 13th overall and **THE World University Rankings 2025** ranking it 34th. In the **U.S. News & World Report Best Global Universities 2024-2025**, it holds the 27th position globally. These aggregate scores reflect strong performance across multiple subject areas, with particular excellence in arts and humanities, social sciences, and life sciences.

By subject, the university achieves top-20 global positions in 12 disciplines according to QS Subject Rankings 2024. **Law and Legal Studies** ranks 11th globally, **Education** ranks 12th, and **Anatomy & Physiology** ranks 14th. The **Melbourne Business School** is consistently ranked in the top 30 worldwide for MBA programs, while the **Melbourne Law School** is considered the best in Australia and among the top 10 in the Commonwealth. For engineering, the university ranks 30th globally in civil and structural engineering, and 40th in computer science and information systems. These subject-level rankings directly influence application volumes, as higher-ranked programs attract proportionally more applicants per available seat.

## Admission Competition by Degree Level

### Undergraduate Entry: ATAR and International Equivalents

For domestic students, entry into undergraduate programs is primarily determined by the Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR). The **Bachelor of Commerce** requires a minimum ATAR of 95.00 for guaranteed entry, while the **Bachelor of Science** requires 91.00. International students must provide equivalent scores from their home country’s secondary education system, such as the International Baccalaureate (IB) with a minimum of 38 points for commerce or the GCE A-Levels with grades ranging from A*AA to AAA depending on the program. The **Bachelor of Arts** is relatively less competitive, requiring an ATAR of 85.00, but still draws high application volumes due to its flexibility and reputation.

The university also uses a **Guaranteed Entry Scheme** for domestic students who meet the published ATAR threshold, but for international applicants, places are allocated on a competitive basis with no guaranteed cut-off. In 2024, the Faculty of Business and Economics reported that 68% of international applicants for the Bachelor of Commerce had ATAR-equivalent scores above 97.00, yet only 42% received offers. This indicates that meeting the minimum threshold does not guarantee admission, and applicants with scores significantly above the published minimum have a stronger chance.

### Graduate Coursework: GPA and Prerequisite Requirements

Graduate programs at the University of Melbourne are highly competitive, particularly in professional fields. The **Juris Doctor (JD)** requires a minimum GPA of 5.5 on a 7.0 scale from a recognised undergraduate degree, plus the Law School Admission Test (LSAT) with a minimum score of 150. However, the median LSAT score for admitted students in 2024 was 165, and the median GPA was 6.2. The **Master of Management (Finance)** requires a minimum GPA of 6.0 in the final year of a relevant undergraduate degree, with preference given to applicants who have completed prerequisites in quantitative subjects.

The **Doctor of Medicine (MD)** is one of the most competitive programs globally. Applicants must achieve a minimum GPA of 5.5 and a Graduate Australian Medical School Admissions Test (GAMSAT) score of at least 50 in each section. In 2024, the median GPA for admitted students was 6.8, and the median GAMSAT overall score was 72. The university received 3,400 applications for 310 places in the MD program, yielding an acceptance rate of 9.1%. International applicants face even tighter competition, as only 30 places are reserved for international students out of approximately 800 international applications.

## Competitive Programs with Highest Demand

### Medicine and Health Sciences

The **Doctor of Medicine (MD)** remains the most competitive program at the University of Melbourne, with an acceptance rate of 9.1% for domestic applicants and approximately 3.8% for international applicants. The **Bachelor of Biomedicine** (undergraduate) requires an ATAR of 94.00 for domestic students, but international applicants with equivalent scores of 97.00 or higher still face a 40% rejection rate. The **Master of Public Health** has a slightly higher acceptance rate of 25%, but the **Master of Nursing Science** requires a minimum GPA of 5.0 and completion of specific prerequisite subjects in human anatomy and physiology.

The **Melbourne Dental School** offers a Doctor of Dental Surgery (DDS) with a 12% acceptance rate. Applicants must have a minimum GPA of 5.5 and achieve a competitive score on the Graduate Australian Dental School Admissions Test (GAMSAT or DAT). In 2024, the median GPA for admitted DDS students was 6.5, and the median DAT score was 22. The program receives approximately 600 applications for 70 places annually.

### Law and Business

The **Melbourne Law School** offers the Juris Doctor (JD) as its primary graduate law degree. The program has an acceptance rate of 18% for domestic applicants and 12% for international applicants. In 2024, the median LSAT score for admitted students was 165, and the median GPA was 6.2. The **Master of Laws (LLM)** is slightly less competitive, with an acceptance rate of 30%, but international applicants must demonstrate English proficiency with an IELTS score of 7.0 (no band below 6.5) and provide evidence of prior legal qualifications.

The **Melbourne Business School** reports that the **Master of Management** suite of programs has an overall acceptance rate of 22%. The **Master of Finance** is the most competitive, with a 15% acceptance rate, requiring a minimum GPA of 6.0 and strong quantitative background. The **Master of Marketing** has a 28% acceptance rate. International students must also submit a personal statement and may be required to attend an interview.

### Engineering and Computer Science

The **Melbourne School of Engineering** offers the **Master of Engineering** with specialisations in civil, mechanical, electrical, and software engineering. The program has an acceptance rate of 25% overall, but the **Master of Computer Science** is more competitive at 18%. Applicants must hold a four-year engineering degree or a three-year degree with relevant prerequisites, and achieve a minimum GPA of 5.5. In 2024, the median GPA for admitted students in computer science was 6.3.

The **Bachelor of Science (Engineering)** pathway requires an ATAR of 91.00 for domestic students, but international applicants with equivalent scores of 95.00 or higher are still subject to competitive selection. The university received 5,200 applications for 1,200 places in the engineering undergraduate cohort in 2024, resulting in a 23% acceptance rate. ## Application Strategy and Selection Criteria

### Holistic Assessment Beyond Grades

The University of Melbourne employs a **holistic admissions process** for many graduate programs, particularly in law, medicine, and business. While minimum GPA and test score thresholds exist, the selection committee also evaluates personal statements, referee reports, and relevant work experience. For the **Juris Doctor**, applicants must submit a personal statement explaining their motivation for studying law, and for the **Doctor of Medicine**, a portfolio of extracurricular activities and community service is required. The **Master of International Relations** requires a 500-word statement of purpose and two academic references.

International applicants should note that English language proficiency is a separate requirement, with minimum IELTS scores of 6.5 (no band below 6.0) for most programs, but 7.0 for law, medicine, and education. The university accepts TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, and Cambridge English qualifications as alternatives. Meeting the minimum English requirement does not guarantee admission, but falling below it will result in automatic rejection.

### Timing and Application Rounds

Most graduate programs at the University of Melbourne operate on a **rolling admissions basis** with multiple rounds. For the 2025 intake, the **Doctor of Medicine** had a single round with applications closing on 31 May 2024. The **Juris Doctor** has three rounds: Round 1 closes 31 August, Round 2 closes 31 October, and Round 3 closes 31 January. Applying in Round 1 is strongly recommended, as the acceptance rate for Round 1 applicants is 22%, compared to 15% for Round 2 and 10% for Round 3.

The **Master of Management** programs have two rounds: Round 1 closes 30 April and Round 2 closes 31 July. International applicants are advised to apply in Round 1 to allow sufficient time for visa processing. The university reports that 65% of offers for the Master of Management (Finance) are made in Round 1. For undergraduate programs, applications through the Victorian Tertiary Admissions Centre (VTAC) close on 30 September for domestic students, while international students can apply directly to the university with a 30 November deadline for Semester 1 entry.

## International Student Demographics and Trends

### Source Countries and Application Patterns

According to the **Australian Government Department of Education (2024 International Student Data)**, the University of Melbourne enrolled 21,700 international students in 2024, representing 42% of its total student population. The top five source countries are China (48% of international enrolments), India (12%), Malaysia (6%), Singapore (5%), and Indonesia (4%). Applications from China have increased by 18% year-on-year, driven by demand for business and information technology programs.

The **Master of Management (Finance)** receives the highest number of international applications, with 3,800 applications for 380 places in 2024. The **Bachelor of Commerce** follows with 5,200 international applications for 650 places. The **Doctor of Medicine** receives 800 international applications for 30 places, making it the most competitive program for international students by acceptance rate (3.8%).

### Visa and Post-Study Work Considerations

International students at the University of Melbourne benefit from Australia’s **Post-Study Work (PSW)** visa framework. Graduates of bachelor’s degrees can obtain a two-year PSW visa, while master’s by coursework graduates receive three years, and master’s by research or doctoral graduates receive four years. From July 2024, the Australian Government extended PSW durations by one year for graduates in specified skill shortage areas, including nursing, engineering, and information technology.

The university’s **Career Development Centre** reports that 89% of international graduates find employment within six months of graduation, with median starting salaries of AUD 75,000 for bachelor’s graduates and AUD 95,000 for master’s graduates. These employment outcomes contribute to the high application volumes, as students perceive the university as a pathway to Australian permanent residency.

## FAQ

### Q1: What are the minimum ATAR or equivalent scores required for international students to enter the University of Melbourne?

International students must provide secondary education qualifications equivalent to an Australian ATAR. For the **Bachelor of Commerce**, the minimum equivalent is an ATAR of 95.00, which translates to an International Baccalaureate (IB) score of 38 or GCE A-Level grades of A*AA. For the **Bachelor of Science**, the minimum is ATAR 91.00 (IB 36 or A-Level AAA). For the **Bachelor of Arts**, the minimum is ATAR 85.00 (IB 33 or A-Level ABB). However, meeting the minimum does not guarantee admission, as places are allocated competitively. In 2024, the median ATAR for admitted international commerce students was 97.20, and 25% of successful applicants had scores above 98.50.

### Q2: How competitive is the Doctor of Medicine (MD) program for international students?

The MD program is the most competitive at the university, with an acceptance rate of approximately 3.8% for international applicants. In 2024, the university received 800 international applications for 30 places. The minimum requirements are a GPA of 5.5 and a GAMSAT score of at least 50 in each section, but the median GPA for admitted international students was 6.8, and the median GAMSAT overall score was 74. International applicants must also demonstrate English proficiency with an IELTS score of 7.0 (no band below 7.0) and submit a portfolio of extracurricular activities.

### Q3: What is the acceptance rate for the Master of Management (Finance) program?

The Master of Management (Finance) has an acceptance rate of 15% for international applicants. In 2024, the program received 3,800 international applications for 380 places. The minimum GPA requirement is 6.0 on a 7.0 scale in the final year of a relevant undergraduate degree, but the median GPA for admitted students was 6.5. Applicants must also complete prerequisite subjects in calculus, linear algebra, and statistics. Applying in Round 1 (closing 30 April) significantly improves chances, as 65% of offers are made in this round.
