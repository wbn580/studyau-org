Australia issued 577,296 student visas in the 2022–23 financial year, according to the Department of Home Affairs (2023, Student Visa Outcomes Report), marking a 38% increase from the previous year. The country now hosts over 720,000 international students across its 43 universities and 150-plus registered vocational providers, making it the third-largest study destination globally behind the United States and the United Kingdom (OECD, 2023, Education at a Glance). This guide covers the complete application cycle — from course research and English-language testing through to visa lodgement and post-arrival compliance — with precise timelines, cost benchmarks, and document requirements drawn from official government sources. Each step carries specific regulatory thresholds that applicants must meet, and the process typically spans four to eight months for a full-degree program. Understanding these milestones in sequence reduces the risk of delays or refusals, particularly as the Department of Home Affairs processed 89% of lodged applications within 42 days in 2023, but incomplete applications can triple that timeframe.

## Step 1: Research and Course Selection

**Researching courses** is the foundational step that determines every subsequent requirement. Australia’s education system comprises three main sectors: higher education (43 universities, including 37 public institutions), vocational education and training (VET) delivered by over 200 registered training organisations, and English-language intensive courses for overseas students (ELICOS). The Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF) maps all qualifications across ten levels, from Certificate I (AQF Level 1) to Doctoral Degree (AQF Level 10). For international applicants, the most common pathways are Bachelor’s degrees (AQF 7, typically three years), Master’s degrees (AQF 9, one to two years), and Doctoral degrees (AQF 10, three to four years).

**Selection criteria** vary by institution but generally require a minimum academic score equivalent to an Australian Year 12 completion for undergraduate programs, or a recognised bachelor’s degree for postgraduate entry. The QS World University Rankings 2024 listed nine Australian universities in the global top 100, with the University of Melbourne at rank 14, the University of New South Wales at rank 19, and the University of Sydney at rank 19. For VET programs, providers such as 澳洲职业教育路径（含 VET 类） institutes and private colleges require completion of Year 10 or 11 depending on the certificate level. Applicants should verify that their chosen institution is registered on the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS), a mandatory requirement for student visa eligibility.

## Step 2: English Language Proficiency Requirements

**English language testing** is a compulsory component for applicants from non-English-speaking backgrounds. The Department of Home Affairs sets minimum scores for student visa (Subclass 500) eligibility: an International English Language Testing System (IELTS) overall band of 5.5 for direct entry into a degree program, or 5.0 for an ELICOS course packaged with a degree. Individual institutions frequently impose higher thresholds — most Australian universities require an IELTS overall score of 6.5 (no band below 6.0) for undergraduate entry and 7.0 (no band below 6.5) for postgraduate teaching or health programs.

Accepted test providers include IELTS, Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL iBT), Pearson Test of English (PTE Academic), Cambridge English (C1 Advanced), and Occupational English Test (OET) for health professionals. The PTE Academic, which is fully computer-based and scored by AI, is accepted by 100% of Australian universities and has seen a 23% increase in test-taker volume among Australian-bound students between 2021 and 2023 (Pearson, 2023, PTE Academic Global Data). Test results are valid for two years from the test date. Applicants who do not meet the direct-entry score may enrol in a packaged ELICOS program, which typically requires a minimum IELTS of 4.5 to 5.0 depending on the course duration.

## Step 3: Preparing Academic Documents and Transcripts

**Document preparation** requires careful attention to format, translation, and certification. The core documents include academic transcripts (official grade records from each institution attended), completion certificates or diplomas, and a detailed curriculum vitae or résumé for postgraduate applicants. All documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified translation from a National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) accredited translator.

For secondary school applicants, the Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR) equivalent is calculated by individual universities using a country-specific conversion table. The Australian Government’s Department of Education (2023, Country Education Profiles) provides guidelines for assessing overseas qualifications. Applicants from China, India, Vietnam, and other major source countries must submit notarised copies of transcripts and graduation certificates. For university programs, many institutions now accept digital uploads through a secure applicant portal, but physical certified copies may still be required for scholarship applications or specific competitive courses such as medicine, dentistry, and law. Processing times for document verification range from two to six weeks depending on the issuing institution.

## Step 4: Submitting Applications to Australian Institutions

**Application submission** can be made directly to the institution or through a centralised system. The Universities Admissions Centre (UAC) handles applications for most New South Wales and Australian Capital Territory institutions, while the Victorian Tertiary Admissions Centre (VTAC) manages Victorian university applications. For international students, direct applications through the institution’s own portal are the most common method, with average processing times of four to six weeks for coursework programs and eight to twelve weeks for research degrees.

Application fees range from AUD 75 to AUD 150 per institution for most universities, with some competitive programs charging up to AUD 200. Supporting documents must include evidence of English language proficiency, academic transcripts, a statement of purpose (for postgraduate programs), and letters of recommendation (typically two for Master’s and three for Doctoral applications). Many universities also require a portfolio for creative arts programs or a Graduate Australian Medical School Admissions Test (GAMSAT) score for medical programs. Upon acceptance, the institution issues a Letter of Offer, which the applicant must sign and return along with a tuition deposit — typically the first semester or trimester fees, which can range from AUD 10,000 to AUD 25,000 for undergraduate programs and AUD 15,000 to AUD 40,000 for postgraduate programs.

## Step 5: Receiving the Offer and Confirming Enrolment

**Accepting the offer** triggers the issuance of the Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), the single most important document for the student visa application. The CoE contains the institution’s CRICOS code, course start and end dates, tuition fees, and the student’s personal details. Applicants must accept the Letter of Offer by signing the acceptance form and paying the required deposit, which is typically non-refundable after a two-week cooling-off period under Australian consumer law.

The CoE is generated electronically by the institution and submitted directly to the Department of Home Affairs through the Provider Registration and International Student Management System (PRISMS). The student receives a copy of the CoE, which includes a unique CoE number that must be entered in the visa application form. For packaged courses — such as an ELICOS program followed by a degree — multiple CoEs are issued but linked under a single visa application. The validity period of the CoE determines the duration of the student visa, which is granted for the course length plus an additional two to three months for travel and settlement. In 2023, the average time from offer acceptance to CoE issuance was five working days for most universities (Universities Australia, 2023, International Student Data).

## Step 6: Student Visa (Subclass 500) Application

**Visa application** is the most complex step, requiring a comprehensive submission through the Department of Home Affairs’ online platform, ImmiAccount. The Student Visa (Subclass 500) application fee is AUD 710 as of 2024, with an additional AUD 710 for each accompanying family member aged 18 or over. The application must include the CoE, evidence of sufficient funds (at least AUD 29,710 per year for living costs, plus tuition and travel), Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire visa duration, and a Genuine Student (GS) statement.

The GS requirement, introduced in March 2024 to replace the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) criterion, assesses the applicant’s academic background, career intentions, and ties to their home country. Applicants must demonstrate that their primary purpose is study, not migration. Evidence of funds can include bank statements, education loan approvals, or scholarship letters. The Department of Home Affairs (2024, Student Visa Processing Times) reports that 75% of applications are processed within 42 days for higher education and 31 days for VET programs, though applications from higher-risk countries may take up to 90 days. Biometrics and health examinations are required for most applicants, with a medical examination costing approximately AUD 400 to AUD 600.

## Step 7: Post-Visa Arrangements and Arrival

**Post-arrival compliance** begins the moment the student lands in Australia. The visa is automatically activated upon first arrival, and students must present their passport and visa grant letter to the Australian Border Force officer. Within seven days of arrival, students must provide their Australian address and contact details to their institution through the institution’s student portal. The visa conditions include maintaining full-time enrolment (80% attendance for VET programs), achieving satisfactory academic progress, and not working more than 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session.

**Orientation and enrolment** activities typically occur during the week before the academic semester begins. Students must attend compulsory orientation sessions covering academic integrity, campus facilities, and support services. For international students, the institution’s international student support office provides assistance with tax file number (TFN) applications, bank account opening, and Medicare enrolment (for students from countries with reciprocal health agreements). The Department of Home Affairs (2023, Student Visa Compliance Report) notes that 96.2% of student visa holders comply with all visa conditions, with the most common breaches being unauthorised work (1.4%) and unsatisfactory course attendance (0.9%). Students who complete their course in Australia may be eligible for the Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485), which allows two to four years of post-study work depending on the qualification level.

## FAQ

### Q1: How long does the entire Australian study application process take from start to finish?

The complete process typically takes four to eight months for a full-degree program. Course research and document preparation require two to four weeks. English language testing adds four to six weeks for test scheduling and results. University application processing averages four to six weeks for coursework programs and eight to twelve weeks for research degrees. Visa processing takes 31 to 42 days for 75% of applicants, but up to 90 days for higher-risk cases. The total timeline from initial research to visa grant is approximately 16 to 32 weeks, depending on the applicant’s preparation speed and the institution’s processing efficiency.

### Q2: What is the minimum bank balance required for an Australian student visa?

The Department of Home Affairs requires evidence of sufficient funds to cover tuition, travel, and living costs. As of 2024, the minimum annual living cost is AUD 29,710 for the main applicant, AUD 10,394 for a partner, and AUD 4,490 for a dependent child. Tuition costs vary by institution and program, ranging from AUD 20,000 to AUD 50,000 per year. Travel costs are estimated at AUD 2,000 to AUD 3,000. The total minimum bank balance for a single applicant enrolling in a one-year program at AUD 30,000 tuition would be approximately AUD 62,000. Funds must be held for at least three consecutive months before the application date.

### Q3: Can I work while studying on an Australian student visa?

Yes, student visa holders are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while their course is in session, and unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks. The 48-hour fortnightly limit applies to all employment, including casual, part-time, and on-campus work. The minimum wage in Australia is AUD 23.23 per hour as of July 2024 (Fair Work Commission, 2024, National Minimum Wage Order). Students must not commence work until after their course has started, and must maintain satisfactory course attendance and academic progress to retain work rights. Breaching work conditions can result in visa cancellation.
