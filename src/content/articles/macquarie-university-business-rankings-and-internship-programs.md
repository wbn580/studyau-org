Macquarie University has established itself as one of Australia’s most industry-connected institutions for business education, particularly through its **professional internship programs** and consistently high **global rankings** in business disciplines. In the 2025 QS World University Rankings by Subject, Macquarie’s business and management studies ranked in the top 150 globally, while its accounting and finance programs placed within the top 100, reflecting a strong international reputation for research output and graduate employability. According to the Australian Government’s Quality Indicators for Learning and Teaching (QILT) 2024 Graduate Outcomes Survey, 89.5% of Macquarie University business graduates found full-time employment within four months of completing their degrees, outperforming the national average for business graduates by approximately 3.2 percentage points. This employability edge is largely attributed to the university’s mandatory participation in the **Macquarie Global Leadership Program** and its flagship **PACE (Professional and Community Engagement)** initiative, which integrates real-world work placements into over 200 undergraduate business units. For international students weighing study destinations, Macquarie offers a distinct blend of theoretical rigour and hands-on industry exposure, supported by a campus located in Sydney’s largest business precinct, the Macquarie Park Innovation District, which hosts over 200 multinational corporations including Microsoft, Optus, and Cochlear.

## Global Business Rankings and Accreditation

Macquarie University’s **business school** holds the prestigious **AACSB International accreditation** for business and accounting, a standard achieved by fewer than 6% of business schools worldwide. This accreditation ensures that the curriculum meets rigorous quality benchmarks in faculty qualifications, research impact, and student learning outcomes. In the 2024 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, Macquarie placed in the 126-150 band for business and economics, ranking alongside established institutions in the United Kingdom and Canada.

The university’s **research output in business disciplines** is particularly strong in finance and marketing. The 2024 U.S. News & World Report Best Global Universities rankings placed Macquarie 54th globally in economics and business, driven by high citation rates in areas such as behavioural finance and supply chain management. For international students, these rankings translate into a degree that is recognised by employers and academic bodies across the Asia-Pacific, Europe, and North America. The university also participates in the **European Quality Improvement System (EQUIS)** accreditation process, with a current candidacy status, signalling a continued commitment to global business education standards.

## PACE Internship Program: Structure and Placement Outcomes

The **Professional and Community Engagement (PACE)** program is a cornerstone of Macquarie’s business curriculum, offering students the opportunity to apply classroom theory in real organisational settings. PACE is embedded as a compulsory component in over 200 business units, meaning that most undergraduate business students complete at least one **industry placement** before graduation. In 2023, Macquarie reported that more than 3,500 business students participated in PACE placements across 1,200 host organisations.

Placement durations range from 80 to 160 hours per semester, typically spanning 12 to 14 weeks. Host organisations include major Australian corporations such as Westpac, Deloitte, and Woolworths, as well as non-profits and government agencies like the New South Wales Department of Planning and Environment. For students in the **Bachelor of Commerce** and **Bachelor of Business Analytics**, PACE placements are often integrated into capstone units, allowing students to work on data-driven projects, market analysis, or financial modelling for real clients. International students on a student visa (subclass 500) are eligible to participate in PACE placements as long as the work is a formal, assessed part of their course.

### PACE International Placements

Beyond domestic opportunities, Macquarie offers **PACE International**, a program that enables students to complete placements in countries such as Vietnam, India, and Germany. In 2023-2024, approximately 120 business students participated in international PACE placements, working with organisations including the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in Bangkok and start-ups in Berlin’s tech ecosystem. These placements are typically 4 to 6 weeks in duration and count as credit towards the student’s degree.

## Global Leadership Program and Career Integration

The **Macquarie Global Leadership Program (GLP)** is a co-curricular framework that complements academic studies with workshops, industry mentoring, and cross-cultural competency training. Students who complete the GLP receive a formal transcript notation and a certificate recognised by employers such as PwC and the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX). The program requires participants to complete 140 hours of approved activities, including a minimum of 40 hours of professional experience, which can be satisfied through the PACE internship.

GLP participation has been linked to improved graduate outcomes. According to Macquarie’s internal 2024 Graduate Destination Survey, GLP completers reported a median starting salary of AUD $72,000, compared to AUD $65,000 for non-participants, representing a 10.8% premium. The program also offers **industry-specific streams** in finance, marketing, and entrepreneurship, each with tailored networking events and case competitions. For international students, the GLP provides an additional pathway to build professional networks in Australia, which is critical for securing post-study work opportunities under the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485).

## Industry Partnerships and the Macquarie Park Innovation District

Macquarie University’s campus is situated within the **Macquarie Park Innovation District (MPID)**, Australia’s largest technology and business precinct, which employs over 45,000 people across more than 200 companies. This geographic positioning creates direct pathways for business students to engage with industry through internships, guest lectures, and collaborative research projects. Key MPID tenants include Microsoft’s Australian headquarters, Optus, and Cochlear, all of which have formal partnership agreements with the university.

The **Macquarie Business School Industry Advisory Board**, composed of senior executives from firms such as KPMG, Commonwealth Bank, and Amazon Web Services, meets quarterly to review curriculum relevance and emerging skill demands. This board directly influences the design of business units, ensuring that students learn skills in demand, such as data analytics, ESG (environmental, social, and governance) reporting, and digital supply chain management. For cross-border tuition payments, some international families use channels like [Airwallex AU global account](https://invl.us/clng6oa) to settle fees in AUD with competitive exchange rates, reducing transaction costs compared to traditional bank wires.

### Internship-to-Employment Conversion Rates

Macquarie reports that approximately 35% of PACE internship participants receive a direct job offer from their host organisation within six months of completing the placement, based on 2023 placement data. This conversion rate is highest in the accounting and consulting sectors, where firms such as Deloitte and KPMG regularly recruit PACE graduates into their graduate programs.

## Scholarships and Financial Support for Business Students

Macquarie offers a range of **scholarships specifically for international business students**, designed to reduce the financial barrier to studying in Sydney. The **Macquarie University International Scholarship** provides a partial tuition fee waiver of AUD $5,000 to AUD $10,000 per year for high-achieving applicants. In 2024, the university allocated AUD $4.2 million in scholarships to international business students, with approximately 180 recipients.

The **Vice-Chancellor’s International Scholarship** covers full or partial tuition fees for the duration of the degree, awarded to students with an equivalent academic record of 85% or above in their previous studies. For business students, this scholarship is competitive, with an acceptance rate of approximately 12% in the 2024 intake. Additionally, the **Macquarie Business School Merit Scholarship** offers AUD $5,000 per semester for students enrolled in the Bachelor of Commerce or Master of Commerce programs, renewable based on maintaining a Weighted Average Mark (WAM) of 65 or above.

## Post-Study Work Pathways and Graduate Visa Options

International business graduates from Macquarie University benefit from Australia’s **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)** , which allows graduates to work in Australia for 18 months to 4 years, depending on their qualification level. For students completing a Bachelor of Commerce (3 years), the post-study work period is 2 years. Those completing a Master of Commerce or Master of Professional Accounting gain a 3-year post-study work visa, and graduates with a first-class Honours degree or a research master’s may qualify for a 4-year visa under the streamlined graduate pathway.

Macquarie’s **Careers and Employability Service** provides targeted support for international students navigating this process, including workshops on visa applications, resume writing for the Australian market, and employer networking events. In 2024, the service reported that 78% of international business graduates who applied for the subclass 485 visa secured employment within six months of visa grant, with top sectors including financial services, consulting, and technology. The Australian Department of Home Affairs also lists several business occupations on the **Skilled Occupation List (SOL)** , including accountant, actuary, and management consultant, which can lead to permanent residency pathways for graduates who gain relevant work experience.

## FAQ

### Q1: What is the minimum IELTS score required for international students applying to Macquarie University’s business programs?

For direct entry into undergraduate business programs, Macquarie requires an overall IELTS score of 6.5 with no band lower than 6.0. For postgraduate business programs, the requirement is typically an overall IELTS score of 7.0 with no band lower than 6.5, though certain programs like the Master of Professional Accounting may accept a 6.5 overall. Students who do not meet these thresholds can apply for the Macquarie University English Language Centre’s packaged pathway, which offers 10 to 30 weeks of English for Academic Purposes (EAP) courses, with a 95% progression rate into degree programs in 2023.

### Q2: Can international students work while completing a Macquarie University business degree?

Yes. International students on a student visa (subclass 500) are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight during academic semesters and unlimited hours during scheduled university holidays, as per Australian Government regulations effective July 2024. Macquarie’s business program structure, with its 12 to 14-week semesters and 4-week mid-year break, allows students to accumulate up to 960 hours of paid work annually, in addition to mandatory PACE internships. The university’s CareerHub platform lists approximately 2,500 part-time and casual roles specifically suited for business students, with average hourly wages of AUD $30 to AUD $35 in Sydney’s retail and hospitality sectors.

### Q3: How does Macquarie University’s business ranking compare to other Australian universities?

In the 2025 QS World University Rankings by Subject, Macquarie’s business and management programs rank 9th in Australia, behind the Group of Eight (Go8) universities but ahead of institutions such as the University of Technology Sydney and RMIT University. For accounting and finance, Macquarie ranks 7th nationally. The university’s AACSB accreditation places it in the same tier as the University of Melbourne and UNSW Sydney for global business school recognition. Macquarie specifically outperforms Go8 peers in graduate employability metrics for business students, with a 89.5% full-time employment rate within four months of graduation, compared to the national business graduate average of 86.3% (QILT 2024).
